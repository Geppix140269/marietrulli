Common Questions Answered

Do I need to live in Italy to qualify for grants?
No, you don't need to be an Italian resident. EU citizens and non-EU investors can qualify for grants. I've helped clients from Paris, London, New York, and Dubai secure funding while managing everything remotely.
What types of properties qualify for grants?
Properties in historic centers (centro storico), masserias, trulli, and buildings for tourism or hospitality use typically qualify. The key is knowing which grants match your property type and structuring the purchase correctly from day one.
How long does the entire process take?
From initial consultation to grant approval typically takes 4-6 months. Brigitte received her €810,000 approval in just 5 months. The key is having all documentation perfect from the start and knowing exactly which officials to work with.
What if my grant application is rejected?
In my track record, this rarely happens because I only take on projects I know will qualify. If there are issues, we address them before submission. My success comes from thorough preparation and deep knowledge of what Regione Puglia actually approves.
Why should I pay you instead of using a local agent?
Local agents work for sellers, not buyers. They earn commissions on sales, not on your grant success. I work exclusively for you, and my success fee is tied to your funding approval. Plus, I speak your language and protect your interests as a fellow international investor.
